Miscelaneous Games


These are a few small projects that didn't quite make it to the prototype stage. Often, I would start working on an idea, develop it to some extent, and then abandon it either because it wasn't enjoyable, didn't align with my vision, or because I struggled to progress further with it. I've selected three of these projects to showcase here, ones that I found interesting enough to share.


Endless runner game


I attempted to create a traditional three lanes endless runner game, where players would need to attack oncoming enemies. I started by implementing a system for generating the infinite map with obstacles randomly distributed across the lanes. However, the side-view perspective, intended to provide clarity on approaching enemies, ironically made it challenging to tell which lane the obstacles were in and my inexperience in programming at the time led me to problems with generating the map.


Little bug game


I started this little project after watching a video about pathfinding in Unity, feeling inspired to give it a shot. I quickly put together a scene with obstacles, a player controller, and a pathfinding agent. In the game, you control the larger bug, while the smaller one follows you around using the pathfinding algorithm.


You can also click anywhere on the screen to send the little bug to that spot. It will autonomously find a path and navigate to the destination without getting stuck on obstacles. Clicking again makes the small bug return to following you. I thought this mechanic could be used to make an interesting puzzle game, but I didn't pursue it further. Perhaps I'll revisit the idea in the future. It was for this project that I originally drew the bug's sprite, at the time I didn't put much thought into it but ended up really liking it and started putting it everywhere I can.


Multiplayer 2D Shooter


This project was born out of inspiration by a video tutorial on creating peer-to-peer multiplayer lobbies using Steam's API. It sets up a lobby linked to your Steam user, allowing you to invite friends to join. The vision I had for this game was to develop a 2D shooter, similar to a mobile game my friends and I used to play called 'Mini Militia'. I wanted the characters to look stylish and have nice animations for moving around and I like what I was able to achieve in that aspect.


Unfortunately, making a multiplayer game proved to be much more difficult and it didn't help that at the time I was very inexperienced in programming and game development, testing multiplayer features was complicated while working on my single laptop. It also introduced many complications about server and client coordination, issues with spawning objects, playing animations, movement and aim of the player characters, and more. Despite all that, I want to try it again in the future, with a better understanding of networking.
