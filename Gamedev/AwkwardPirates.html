Awkward Pirates


Awkward Pirates was developed as an entry for GameJam+ 2024 in under 3 days. From the theme "Throwback Thrill" we created a game in the aesthetic medium "Pixel Art". It is about 4 pirates and Captain Pascual, whose goal is to reach mainland without their ship sinking. Participating on GameJam+ was an amazing experience and such a great time!

Made in collaboration with the amazing team I had:

Aylan Miranda, with the heavy lifting on the programming. Follow Aylan's Gamedev account.
Alvaro Moya, responsible for the sound design. Follow Alvaro's Photography account.
Elena Guillén, the artist for the background and scenary props. Follow Elena's art account.
Lucas Corrales, who designed the characters and animated VFX. Follow Lucas' art account.




After the themes for the game jam were revealed we got to thinking fast about potential ideas and concepts for our game. From before hand we had agreed on themes and games we did and did not want to make.
We quickly agreed on a game with a somewhat grim atmosphere and we got inspired to make the scenario a boat where you would face threats from fantastical sea creatures. We went back and forth with different ideas until we thought of making a resource manager kind of game where you would have to order your tripulation around to do tasks that helped the ship move and other tasks to fix the boat to avoid it sinking.

Aylan and I quickly got to building the systems we would need for the gameplay loop while Lucas started drawing the characters and Elena sketched the background for the play area.
The main gameplay consists of clicking on the crew members to assign them to different tasks like rowing, fixing the sail and fixing water leaks.

Eventually you will face a mermaid who will hipnotize your tripulation and kill them if you don't act fast enough, leaving you impaired and sure to sink if you lose everyone.
We had planned for more encounters and tasks but unfortunately, under the time limit, we had to cut content, which is totally normal on game jams.


You will get a game over screen if your boat sinks or a game won screen if you manage to beat the game. Despite being mainly a programmer, I took the time to make these illustrations and I think they gave the game some charm.


This was only the first phase of GameJam+. After this we had to make a video pitch and continue working towards a complete game.
Our team stuck togheter for the first few months, but unfortunately, due to our schedules and responsibilities we decided to stop working on the game and drop out of the second phase. We had a lot of fun and who knows, we might work togheter again in the future.

Participating on this game jam was an amazing experience, not only because of what we managed to make, but also interacting with the community, talking with the other teams' developers and artists, and taking a look at their work was one of the best parts of the event. There were so many talented and fun people working on games.
I have to give a shoutout to Rodolfo Mora-Zamora for organizing the event and being the head of the CiDeV community. He is one of the great people pushing for the growth of game development in Costa Rica.
