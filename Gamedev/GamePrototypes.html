Game Prototypes


These are some game prototypes on which I worked but didn't get to release. Working on these projects helped me learn the Unity engine and provided me with strong foundations for my future endeavors.


Ball that jumps


This is a small game, but it's the first one I've properly finished. After experimenting with the Unity Engine for some time, I made the decision to embark on and complete a small project, complete with a proper title screen, levels, and gameplay loop.

At the time I had no skill at drawing and making assets, so the visuals are only made of simple shapes.

The gameplay is simple: reach the goal marked by a blue rectangle. To move around, you hold the mouse button, causing the ball to rotate. A triangle on the ball indicates the direction. When the player releases the mouse button, the ball jumps in the direction it's pointing. For finer control over rotation, the player can use both mouse buttons. The right-click rotates the ball clockwise, while the left button rotates it counter-clockwise. Holding both buttons keeps the rotation still, allowing the player to 'confirm' their shot.

The game ended up featuring a total of 10 levels. The final level resembled a 'Boss fight,' where the player had to reach three different goal markers while avoiding the boss' attacks.

This game could have been expanded by adding more levels, but I kept the scope small and considered it finished. I shared the game with some friends, and we had a lot of fun playing it. Little did I know at the time, this game would set me on the path to becoming a hobbyist game developer.


Ball jumping over it


Some time after finishing 'Ball that jumps', I had an idea for an easy-to-make sequel. Heavily inspired by 'Getting Over It with Bennett Foddy', I began developing a game about climbing a mountain using the movement mechanics from my previous game.

A part of the game I wanted to replicate is how punishing it is to miscalculate a movement and falling down to the beginning.


In 'Getting over it' the narrator points out how the level is built from premade assets he just downloaded from the internet, but when put togheter for the game, they formed the world, the mountain for the player to climb.

While my game didn't aim to deliver such profound messages as Bennett Foddy's, it did inspire me to utilize my own accumulated 'trash' assets. So I fine tuned the phyiscs and controls to make them feel juicier and started building a level made up with various assets and images I had made for other projects.

I added elements like moving platforms, fake platforms, effectors and a system to play audio at different points. The mountain consisted of various characters, obstacles, and art I had drawn myself.

I built a big portion of the game, but never quite finished the mountain, funnily enough, I didn't have enough trash to make it very long. I may return to it later with way more trash.


Imagink


In 2023, a friend who's also passionate about game development, introduced me to a game studio he worked for, T3AMPO, who were looking for a programmer to build a prototype of a game they wanted to make.

They had already started development but my friend, who was the previous developer, was needed to work on another project of theirs, so they hired me for approximately two months to assist with the ongoing development.


The game is an 'Adventure platformer that combines intuitive puzzle solving with the power of imagination'. The player has to draw their weapon on a canvas, and different stats are given to it depending on the amount of ink used and number of strokes.

On my time working with them I programmed the basis for a combat system, enemy behaviour and the ability to bring drawn objects into the world to solve puzzles.

Sadly, I had to leave the development team because I was very busy with university. The studio hired some more programmers and I got to meet them and answer questions about the code. Overall the experience was very enriching.

The studio showcased Imagink at GameJam+ Brazil 2023 and secured funding from a sponsor after winning various prizes.

They continued development and eventually realeased the game. You can find it on itch.io.
