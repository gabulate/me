Structured Defenders


Structured Defenders is a Survivors-like game where you place structures to defend yourself! Slay enemies and pick up XP to level up and get more structures and items.




This game was heavily influenced by Vampire Survivors, by Poncle. A game where you slay hordes of enemies with weapons that passively attack on their own while you just walk around collecting XP to get more weapons to slay more enemies.
I thought about combining this idea with Tower Defense games, where you place different units that also attack on their own. In typical Tower Defense games, the player has total vision of the playable area and have to make sure the enemies don't reach a certain point or object. But for this game, the enemies go after the player who controls and moves around as a physical character so they have to play around their units and aren't as free to move around the map.

I liked this idea because it was very simple and manageable as a single developer. So I started working on it around November 2023. I developed systems for placing and upgrading structures and passive items that effect the statistics of the placed structures. Each structure and item exists as a Scriptable Object which can be easily modified to set different statistics at different levels.

One of the other major systems I had to make was the player leveling up and getting a random list of available items to choose from. This wasn't very complicated but I did have to consider scenarios like not offering passive items that have already been maxed out, if all the passive item slots are filled, only show items that the player already has, not offer structures if the player has 4 or more structures in their inventory, only offer items the player has unlocked and if no items are eligible, the player just gets some extra coins.

Another major system was the spawning of enemies, in which my game differs a little from Vampire Survivors. For starters, the enemies won't de-spawn (Although there is a limit to the maximum amount of enemies), no matter how far the player gets from them. The order of the enemies is random, but it is divided in waves and will always spawn the same amount of enemies, and the velocity of the spawner will speed up or slow down depending on how the player is doing, so you could last the same amount of time on two matches but have very different scores.

The part that definitely took the most time was making all the assets. Every sprite you see in the game was drawn by myself. I went for pixel art to make it simpler and easier to maintain a coherent style. I had previous experience with digital art, but I had never tried pixel art, at least for a project on this scale. The pixel art style also let me get away with making most animations be only 3 or 4 frames.
Still, even with these creative restrictions, drawing every sprite was the most time consuming part of this project. I'm no professional artist, but I think the overall aesthetic turned out okay.

For the enemies included in the game (aside from the little bug creatures) I decided to draw inspiration from costa rican and latin-american culture. "Duendes", "La Tulevieja", "El Sisimiqui", "El Padre sin cabeza", "La Llorona" and my personal favorite; "La Segua" do as the enemies that the player has to fight off.

There's also different characters that the player can unlock and play as, with the only differences between them being their appearance and their starting item.

This game could be endlessly expanded by adding more structures, more characters, more enemies, more levels, more content...

But after about 6 months of development, I really wanted to move on to other projects, so I decided to wrap up development of new content and polish the game for release. This of course meant cutting ideas and other things I wanted to include, but if I felt that I would never finish the game if I didn't. I also didn't want to be stuck in the same project and end up hating or resenting it, after all, since the beginning I set myself to make something simple, and this is also just my first game!

I started development in November 2023 and officially finished it in July 2024 by working on it on my free time.
In the end, I learned a lot and even with all the difficulties. I'm proud of this little silly game I managed to make and share with others and it has given me a lot of inspiration for my future projects!
